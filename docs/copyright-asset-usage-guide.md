## The Golden Rule

**When in doubt, don't use it.** If you didn't create it from scratch or obtain explicit written permission, assume you cannot use it.

---

## Copyright Basics

### What is Copyright?
- Automatic legal protection for original creative works
- **No registration required** - exists the moment something is created
- Protects: Music, images, video, text, code, logos, designs
- Duration: Life of creator + 70 years (or 95 years for corporate works)

### What CAN'T You Do Without Permission?
- ❌ Copy someone else's work
- ❌ Distribute it (even for free)
- ❌ Display it publicly
- ❌ Create derivative works
- ❌ Use it in commercial projects

### What CAN You Do?
- ✅ Use works in the **Public Domain** (expired copyright or explicitly released)
- ✅ Use works with **CC0, CC-BY, or compatible Creative Commons licenses**
- ✅ Use **licensed stock assets** you purchased
- ✅ Use **royalty-free assets** from reputable sources (check license terms)

## Logos & Brand Assets

### Can I Use the SEGA Logo?

**Short Answer:** No, not without permission.

**Why:**
- SEGA logo is a **registered trademark**
- Trademarks protect brand identity and commercial use
- Even non-commercial use can imply endorsement

**Legal Risk:**
- **Trademark infringement** - even if you don't profit
- **Brand dilution** - using it incorrectly weakens their brand
- **Cease & Desist** letter (likely)
- **Lawsuit** (rare but possible, especially if they think you're profiting)

**Exception - Fair Use:**
- News reporting about SEGA
- Educational content analyzing SEGA's branding
- Parody (very narrow, risky)

**Safe Alternative:**
- Create your own retro-styled logo
- Use open-source game-inspired icons from:
  - [Game-Icons.net](https://game-icons.net) (CC BY 3.0)
  - [SVGRepo](https://www.svgrepo.com) (check individual licenses)
  - Custom vector art you create

## Music & Audio

### Can I Use Copyrighted Music?

**Short Answer:** Only if you have explicit permission or license.

**Common Misconceptions:**
| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "It's only 30 seconds, so it's fair use" | ❌ **False** - Any amount can be infringement |
| "I'm not making money from it" | ❌ **False** - Non-commercial use is still infringement |
| "I'll give credit to the artist" | ❌ **False** - Credit doesn't replace permission |
| "It's a cover, so it's my version" | ❌ **False** - Covers need mechanical licenses |
| "It's on YouTube, so it's public" | ❌ **False** - YouTube doesn't own the copyright |

**Where People Get Sued:**
- Using popular songs as background music
- Cover versions without license
- Remixes without permission (even if "transformative")
- Music in YouTube videos (Content ID detects everything)

### YouTube Content ID System

**How it works:**
1. Your video uploads
2. Content ID scans audio
3. **Match found:**
   - Video blocked worldwide
   - Monetization goes to copyright holder
   - **Strike on your channel** (3 strikes = channel deleted)

**Real Example:**
- Using 10 seconds of a Disney song → **Video blocked in 247 countries**
- Using Mario theme music → **Content ID claim, Nintendo monetizes your video**
- Using any mainstream music → **95% chance of automated detection**

### Safe Music Sources

**Free (with attribution):**
- [YouTube Audio Library](https://www.youtube.com/audiolibrary/music)
- [Free Music Archive](https://freemusicarchive.org) (check license)
- [Incompetech](https://incompetech.com) (CC BY)

**Royalty-Free (paid, one-time):**
- [Epidemic Sound](https://www.epidemicsound.com)
- [Artlist](https://artlist.io)
- [PremiumBeat](https://www.premiumbeat.com)

**Creative Commons:**
- **CC0:** Can use freely (no attribution)
- **CC-BY:** Can use with attribution
- **CC-BY-SA:** Can use with attribution + share-alike
- **❌ CC-NC:** Cannot use commercially (avoid!)
- **❌ CC-ND:** Cannot make derivatives (avoid!)

## Images & Video

### Can I Use That Screenshot from Google?

**No.**

**Why:**
- Images are copyrighted automatically
- "Right-click, save as" = copyright infringement
- Using it = public distribution

**Where People Get Caught:**
- Background images from Pinterest
- Stock photos without paying
- Memes using copyrighted characters (Disney, Nintendo, Pixar)
- Movie/TV screenshots
- Game screenshots

**Safe Image Sources:**
- [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com) (free, no attribution)
- [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com) (free, no attribution)
- [Pixabay](https://pixabay.com) (free, check license)
- [Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org) (check license)
- Screenshots you take yourself (but don't show copyrighted content)

**Special Note on Game Screenshots:**

**Game publishers' policies vary:**
- **Nintendo:** Very strict, frequently issues takedowns
- **Microsoft/Xbox:** More lenient, allows screenshots for content
- **Sony/PlayStation:** Middle-ground, allows with attribution
- **Indie publishers:** Usually okay, check their terms

**Best practice:** If showing game screenshots, use:
- Your own gameplay footage
- Not the main "marketing" images
- Transform it (add commentary, analysis, education)

## Fair Use vs. Transformative Work

### What Is Fair Use?

Four factors courts consider:

1. **Purpose & character**
   - ✅ Educational, news, commentary, criticism, parody
   - ❌ Commercial use, entertainment

2. **Nature of copyrighted work**
   - ✅ Factual content
   - ❌ Highly creative works (music, art, movies)

3. **Amount used**
   - ✅ Small portion
   - ❌ The "heart" of the work

4. **Effect on market**
   - ✅ Doesn't harm original's market
   - ❌ Replaces need for original

### The YouTube "Transformative" Myth

**YouTube creators often say:**
"My video is transformative because I added commentary!"

**Reality:**
- **Transformative is a high bar** - must fundamentally change purpose
- React videos → **Often NOT transformative** (still entertainment)
- Analysis videos → **More likely transformative** (educational purpose)
- Video essays → **Most likely transformative** (critical commentary)

**Example:**
- ❌ Uploading full movie with "commentary" → **Copyright infringement**
- ✅ Analyzing movie's cinematography with clips → **Transformative (maybe)**
- ✅ Critiquing game's design with gameplay → **Transformative (maybe)**

**Risk:** Even if you're right, you may need to:
- Fight Content ID claim
- Hire lawyer ($5,000-$50,000)
- Go to court (years of litigation)

## Worst-Case Scenarios

### What COULD Happen (from least to worst)

**Level 1: Automated Detection**
- Content ID claim (YouTube)
- DMCA takedown notice
- Video/page taken down
- **Resolution:** Remove content, submit counter-claim (risky)

**Level 2: Cease & Desist**
- Formal letter from company's lawyers
- Demand to remove content within X days
- Threat of lawsuit
- **Resolution:** Comply immediately, don't respond unless you have a lawyer

**Level 3: Copyright Strike / Account Penalty**
- YouTube: 3 strikes = channel deleted
- GitHub: Repository disabled
- Social media: Account suspended
- **Resolution:** Usually permanent

**Level 4: Lawsuit**
- Company sues for copyright infringement
- **Damages:** $750-$30,000 PER WORK (statutory)
- **If willful:** Up to $150,000 PER WORK
- **Legal fees:** $5,000-$50,000+ for defense
- **Timeline:** 1-3 years in court
- **Resolution:** Settlement (expensive) or judgment (potentially bankrupting)

### Real Examples:

**YouTuber used 30 seconds of a song:**
- Settlement: **$3,500 + remove video**
- Legal fees: **$8,000**
- **Total cost: $11,500**

**Developer used stock photo without license:**
- Getty Images lawsuit
- Settlement: **$1,200**
- Could have been: **$5,000-$10,000**

**Small business used Disney characters in logo:**
- Disney cease & desist
- Had to rebrand everything
- **Cost: $15,000+ in design, printing, marketing**

## How to Protect Yourself

### Before Using ANY Asset:

1. **Ask yourself:**
   - Did I create this from scratch? ⭐ **YES = Use it**
   - Do I have a written license/permission? ⭐ **YES = Use it**
   - Is it CC0 or CC-BY with attribution? ⭐ **YES = Use it**
   - Is it royalty-free stock with license? ⭐ **YES = Use it**
   - **NO to all = DON'T USE IT**

2. **Document everything:**
   - Save license emails
   - Screenshot terms of use
   - Keep receipts for stock assets

3. **When in doubt:**
   - Email/call the copyright holder
   - Get explicit written permission
   - Or: Use something else

### If You Get a Takedown Notice:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Ignore it
- ❌ Fight back without a lawyer
- ❌ Complain publicly (can make it worse)
- ❌ Use the content anyway

**DO:**
- ✅ Remove content IMMEDIATELY
- ✅ Save all documentation
- ✅ Consult a lawyer if serious
- ✅ Learn from mistake

## Safe Alternatives

### Instead of Copyrighted Music:
- **Epidemic Sound / Artlist** ($15/mo unlimited)
- **YouTube Audio Library** (free)
- **Hire composer on Fiverr** ($50-$500)
- **Ask indie artists** (many allow use with attribution)

### Instead of Brand Logos:
- **Create your own** (Figma, Illustrator)
- **Use generic icons** (Heroicons, Feather Icons)
- **Commission custom logo** (Fiverr, Upwork)
- **Use emojis** (automatically licensed)

### Instead of Stock Photos:
- **Unsplash / Pexels** (free)
- **Take photos yourself**
- **Ask friends/family** (get written permission!)
- **AI generated images** (check platform's terms)

### Instead of Game Screenshots:
- **Use press kit images** (many publishers provide)
- **Use public domain games** (Pong, Tetris - check!)
- **Create mockups** (Figma, Photoshop)
- **Ask indie devs** (often happy for exposure)

## Brand-Specific Guidelines

### Disney
**Policy:** Extremely aggressive protection

**What happens if you use Disney content:**
- **Within days:** Automated DMCA takedown
- **Within weeks:** Cease & desist from lawyers
- **Possible:** Lawsuit (they have unlimited legal budget)

**Don't use:**
- Disney characters (Mickey, Elsa, Marvel, Star Wars)
- Disney music
- Disney movie clips
- Disney fonts

**Risk level:** 🔴 EXTREME - They WILL find you

### Nintendo
**Policy:** Very strict, especially for Mario/Zelda/Pokémon

**Don't use:**
- Nintendo game music
- Character art
- Game sprites
- Official artwork

**Acceptable:**
- Your own gameplay footage with commentary/analysis
- Reviews with short clips (fair use, but risky)

**Risk level:** 🔴 HIGH

### SEGA
**Policy:** Moderately strict

**Don't use without permission:**
- SEGA logo
- Sonic/character art
- Game music
- Official marketing materials

**Better:**
- Create original retro-style graphics inspired by (not copying) SEGA
- Use stock retro gaming icons

**Risk level:** 🟡 MEDIUM

### Microsoft / Xbox
**Policy:** More lenient

**Generally allowed:**
- Gameplay screenshots and video
- Xbox interface screenshots for reviews

**Don't use:**
- Xbox logo in your branding
- Copyrighted music from games
- Official marketing materials

**Risk level:** 🟢 LOW (but still get permission for logos)

### Sony / PlayStation
**Policy:** Middle ground

**Generally allowed:**
- Gameplay footage with commentary
- Reviews with clips

**Don't use:**
- PlayStation logo
- Official soundtracks
- Marketing materials

**Risk level:** 🟡 MEDIUM

### Google / YouTube
**Policy:** Algorithmic enforcement

**Content ID will detect:**
- Any copyrighted music (even 3 seconds)
- Movie clips
- TV show clips
- Video game footage (varies by publisher)

**Consequences:**
- Video blocked
- Monetization transferred to copyright holder
- Copyright strike (3 = channel deletion)

**Risk level:** 🔴 EXTREME (automated detection)

## Checklist Before Publishing

### Every Asset You Use:

- [ ] I created this from scratch **OR**
- [ ] I have a written license/permission **OR**
- [ ] It's CC0 / Public Domain **OR**
- [ ] It's royalty-free stock with proof of purchase
- [ ] I have documented the source and license

### For Each Project:

- [ ] All images are licensed or created by me
- [ ] All music/sounds are licensed or created by me
- [ ] No brand logos used without permission
- [ ] No copyrighted characters or art
- [ ] Attribution given where required
- [ ] I have license documentation saved

### Legal Safety:

- [ ] I have a backup plan if content gets taken down
- [ ] I can afford to remove/replace infringing content quickly
- [ ] I have a lawyer or legal resource (if serious project)
- [ ] I understand fair use is a defense, not a right
- [ ] I know that "giving credit" doesn't replace a license

## Final Thoughts

### Why This Matters

- Legal battles are expensive ($10,000-$500,000+)
- Copyright strikes can delete your channel/account
- Reputational damage if seen as "stealing"
- It's ethically right to compensate creators

### The Real Cost of "Free"

**"Free" music that gets claimed:**
- Your video earns $2,000
- Copyright holder claims it
- **You get $0, they get $2,000**

**"Free" image that gets DMCA'd:**
- Your site has 100k monthly visitors
- Get DMCA for hero image
- Must redesign entire site in 24 hours
- **Cost: 40 hours of work + lost revenue**

**"Free" logo that violates trademark:**
- Build brand for 2 years
- Trademark holder sends C&D
- Must rebrand everything
- **Cost: $50,000+ in rebranding**

### Better Approach

**Spend $50 on a stock photo instead of:**
- Risking $5,000+ legal fees
- Risking DMCA takedown
- Risking account termination
- Risking reputational damage

**Spend $15/month on music instead of:**
- Risking Content ID claims
- Risking demonetization
- Risking channel strikes
- Wasting time disputing claims
